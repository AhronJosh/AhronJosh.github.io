What is a Transportation Problem?

The transportation problem is a classic optimization challenge in operations research. It involves finding the most cost-effective way to transport goods from multiple sources (e.g., plants or factories) to multiple destinations (e.g., warehouses or customers), given supply constraints at sources and demand requirements at destinations.

Key Concepts

  • Sources and Supplies: Origins of goods with limited availability.
  • Destinations and Demands: Endpoints with required quantities.
  • Costs: The cost per unit to transport from a source to a destination.
  • Feasibility: Total supply must equal total demand for a balanced problem.

Methods for Initial Solutions

To solve transportation problems, we often start with an initial feasible solution using methods like:

  • Northwest Corner Method (NWCM): Allocates from the top-left corner of the cost matrix, moving right and down. It's simple but may not be optimal.
  • Least Cost Method (LCM): Allocates to the lowest-cost cells first. It often provides a better starting point for optimization.

After finding an initial solution, you can use techniques like the MODI method or stepping-stone method to reach the optimal solution.
